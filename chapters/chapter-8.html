Chapter VIII

Proverbs on Money, Markets, and the Love of Gold

1Money is a good servant and a ruinous master. The man who forgets which role it plays will, in time, remember. 2The market knows the price of everything and the value of nothing. Do not confuse the two, lest you sell what cannot be repurchased. 3He who chases wealth for its own sake is like a man running toward the horizon — he moves, but the horizon moves with him, and he arrives nowhere. 4Enough is a word that the greedy man cannot pronounce. Teach yourself to say it, and half your suffering is ended. 5Give generously while you are living and can see the good it does. Dead men's legacies serve the lawyers first.
