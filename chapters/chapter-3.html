Chapter III

The Lamentation for His Mother and Father

1How shall I speak of them? For the words that come are not enough, and the words that would be enough I do not have. 2My mother was a woman of quiet strength, who bore her burdens without show, and loved without condition, and asked for little in return, and deserved more than she received. 3My father was a man of his time, shaped by forces that came before him, and I have spent my years trying to understand what he could not say. 4They are gone now, or going, and the world they inhabited — with its certainties and its customs — goes with them. And I am left standing at the edge of it. 5Grieve not as those who have no hope, and yet grieve. For grief is the tax we pay on love, and to refuse it is to deny that what we had was real.
