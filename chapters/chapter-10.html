Chapter X

A Letter to His Child, Not Yet Born

1I do not know your name. I do not know your face. And yet I have already begun to love you, which is a strange and marvellous thing, and I have been turning it over in my hands like a stone that catches the light differently each time. 2I will not pretend the world you are entering is simple or kind. It is neither. But it is full of beauty that the cynics have not managed to extinguish, and that beauty is yours to find. 3I will try to teach you the things that matter, and to keep from teaching you the things that only seemed to matter in my time and will seem small in yours. 4Be curious. Be kind where kindness costs you nothing, and where it costs you something, be kind anyway. Forgive what you can. Remember that I, too, was learning. 5And know this above all: you were wanted. You were anticipated. You were written for, before you were born.
