Chapter VI

The Chronicles of the Digital Age

1And it came to pass in the latter part of the twentieth century and the beginning of the twenty-first that a great network was woven across the face of the earth, and men called it the internet, and marvelled at it. 2And at first it was a place of wonder and of learning, where knowledge was shared freely and the distance between people was made small. 3But in time the merchants came, and after the merchants came the lords of attention, who learned that fear and anger kept the eyes upon the screen longer than truth or beauty did. 4And the people were divided into camps, and each camp was shown only what it already believed, and each came to think its enemies were greater than they were, and its own virtue greater than it was. 5Stephen witnessed these things, and wrote them down, not in condemnation but in record, that those who come after might know what it was to live in the first age of the network.
