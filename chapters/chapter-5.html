Chapter V

A Letter to the Engineers and Builders of This Age

1To you who build the systems upon which the world now runs — you who write the code, lay the cable, design the circuit and the algorithm — these words are set down for you. 2You have been given a power that the ancients did not possess, and with it a responsibility they did not face. Do not pretend otherwise. 3Every system you build will be used by someone you did not imagine, in a way you did not intend, at a time you did not foresee. Build accordingly. 4Do not worship the elegant solution at the expense of the human it was meant to serve. The tool exists for the hand, not the hand for the tool. 5And if the thing you have built causes harm, do not look away from it. Look directly at it, name it plainly, and repair what can be repaired.
