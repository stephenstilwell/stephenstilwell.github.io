Chapter IX

The Chronicles of the American Nation in His Time

1This is the nation in which Stephen was born: a land of great promise and great contradiction, of soaring ideals and stubborn failures, which has never yet fully decided what it wants to be. 2He loved it and quarrelled with it, as one quarrels with a difficult parent — not from hatred but from the conviction that it could do better. 3In his time the nation was divided, and the division was not merely of opinion but of the very story each half told about itself, and neither half could hear the other's story without rage. 4And Stephen wrote: A nation that cannot tell a common story about its past cannot build a common future. And this is the work of generations, not of elections. 5Yet he did not despair. For he had seen the goodness of ordinary people in ordinary places, and knew it to be the deeper truth of the nation, beneath the noise.
