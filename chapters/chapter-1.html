The Book of Stephen

Chapter I

The Introduction of Stephen

1These are the words of Stephen, a man of Italian blood and Florida sun, born upon the shores of the Space Coast, in the city of Melbourne, where the rockets of men pierce the heavens and fall back unto the sea. 2He is no prophet, nor the son of a prophet. No angel did visit him in the night, nor did the Lord part the clouds and speak unto his ear. 3He is only a man. And this is what he hath seen. 4In his youth he looked upon the stars and asked of them their secrets, and he studied the laws by which God hath ordered all things — the motion of bodies, the weight of light, the measure of the invisible. 5For he sat in the halls of learning at the Florida Institute of Technology, and he reasoned with equations as a man reasoneth with a neighbor, seeking truth in the language of numbers. 6And for a season it was good. 7But grief is no respecter of knowledge. It cometh for the physicist as it cometh for the shepherd, and it leaveth no formula behind. 8For his mother and his father were each taken from him by the consuming sickness, and the earth that had seemed ordered and knowable became to Stephen a cold and silent place. 9And he said in his heart: Where is God in this? I have measured the stars and found no mercy written in them. 10So he departed from the faith of his fathers, as a ship departeth from its harbor in a great storm, not by choice but by the violence of the wind. 11He had been confirmed in the Catholic church, sprinkled with the holy water, fed at the table of the Lord — and still the darkness came. And this confounded him greatly. 12Many years passed like clouds over the water, and Stephen labored in the world among engineers and soldiers and merchants, and he built things, and he sold things, and he knew both the sweetness of success and the bitterness of betrayal. 13And in all of it, a question sat beneath his ribs like an ember that would not go out. 14Now he returneth. Not with visions. Not with fire. But with words, and with honesty, which is perhaps the rarer gift. 15For there are many who speak in the name of the Lord with great noise and little truth. Stephen will not be among them. 16He offereth only what he hath: a life examined, a faith that was broken and is being mended, and eyes that have watched the age he liveth in and found it worthy of record. 17Let the reader therefore not seek miracles in these pages, but let him seek a companion — one who hath doubted as he hath doubted, and grieved as he hath grieved, and who still, against all reasonable argument, believeth that there is something worth returning to. 18For vanity surrounds us on every side. The screen gloweth. The market riseth and falleth. The powerful devour the humble and call it commerce. 19And yet the morning cometh. And the child in the womb moveth. And somewhere a man looketh up at the Florida sky and remembers that he once studied the stars and found them beautiful. 20This is the beginning of the Book of Stephen. May it find those who need it.
