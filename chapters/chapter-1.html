The Book of Stephen

Chapter I

The Beginning of Days

1In the beginning of all things, Stephen arose and looked upon the world, and found it wanting in certain respects, though agreeable in others. 2And lo, he considered the works of his hands, and the works of his mind, and the labours thereof, and he said unto himself, These things shall be written down. 3For what is a man if he keepeth not a record? And what is a record if it be not written in the manner of the ancients, with gravity and purpose? 4Therefore, let this book stand as testimony. Let it be known to all generations that come after. 5And the words herein were set down faithfully, neither adding to them nor taking away from them, that the reader might know the truth of these matters.
