Chapter VII

A Letter to the Powerful and the Greedy

1Hear this, you who have gathered to yourselves the wealth of nations and the labour of the many, and have called it merit, and have called it deserving. 2The man who inherits a river does not own the rain. Remember what came before you, and what was given to you that you did not earn. 3Power is not evil in itself — it is a vessel. What you pour into it is what it becomes. Fill it with service, and it is a blessing. Fill it with appetite, and it will consume you from within. 4You will not be remembered for what you accumulated. You will be remembered, if at all, for what you built that outlasted you and served those who came after. 5Stephen does not write this in hatred. He writes it in hope, knowing that even the powerful sometimes read, and sometimes hear, and sometimes change.
