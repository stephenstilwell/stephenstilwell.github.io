Chapter IV

On Doubt, and Why It Is Not Sin

1There are those who say that doubt is the enemy of faith, but Stephen considered this and found it wanting. For doubt is the proving of faith, as fire is the proving of gold. 2The man who has never doubted has never truly believed. He has only repeated what was handed to him, and called the repetition belief. 3Doubt is the honest accounting of the soul. It says: I do not yet fully understand. And this is not failure. This is the beginning of wisdom. 4Therefore let no man be ashamed of his questions. Let him carry them openly, and seek, and not cease from seeking, though the answer be slow in coming. 5For the door does not open to the man who stands at a distance and declares it open. It opens to the man who walks forward and knocks upon it.
