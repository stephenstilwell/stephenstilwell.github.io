Chapter II

The Proverbs of Stephen on Work and Men

1A man who labours without purpose is like a millstone that grinds but has no grain beneath it — much noise, much heat, and nothing made. 2Do not envy the man who does not work, for idleness is a slow ruin, and he is already inside it. 3The craftsman who takes pride in small things will one day be trusted with great ones. The man who scorns small things will never arrive at great ones. 4Beware the man who speaks always of what he will do. Watch instead the man who speaks little and does much. 5Work done in anger is work that must be done again. Work done with patience endures beyond the man who did it.
