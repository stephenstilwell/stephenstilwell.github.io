Chapter XI

On Returning — Of Faith Found Again in the Ruins

1There was a season in which Stephen walked away from the things he had been given to believe, and he did not walk away in anger but in the honest exhaustion of a man who has carried something a long way and can no longer say what it is. 2And in the wilderness of that season he found not emptiness but silence. And in the silence, after a long time, he began again to hear. 3What he came back to was not the same as what he had left. It was smaller in some ways and larger in others. The walls had come down, but the foundation remained. 4He did not return because he had resolved his doubts. He returned because he had learned to live with them, and found that a faith that survives its doubts is stronger than one that was never tested. 5And he said: I do not know everything. I do not need to know everything. I know enough to take the next step, and the next step is what is asked of me.
