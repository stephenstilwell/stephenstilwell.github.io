The Book of Stephen

Chapter XII

The Benediction of Stephen

1And so the book cometh to its close, as all things close, as all men close — not with everything resolved, but with enough said to make the saying worthwhile. 2Stephen is no apostle. He is no saint, though the Church of his fathers hath a patron by his name, and he hath tried in his own imperfect way to honor the bearing of it. 3He is a man of the Space Coast, who looked up at rockets in his boyhood and felt in his chest that the world was larger than what he could see, and who hath spent his life attempting to live accordingly. 4He hath loved. He hath lost. He hath doubted and returned. He hath worked with his hands and his mind and his mouth, and he hath been cheated and he hath persevered, and he hath found that perseverance is less a virtue of the strong than a necessity of the honest. 5A child is coming. A morning is coming. These two facts alone are sufficient reason to continue. 6To the reader who hath come this far — Stephen seeth thee. Thou didst not have to stay, and thou didst, and that meaneth something, even if neither of us can say precisely what. 7May thy grief be finite and thy love be stubborn. May thy doubts lead thee deeper in rather than further away. May thy work be worthy of the hours it consumeth and thy rest be worthy of the work. 8May thou find at least one person who telleth thee the truth without cruelty and remaineth when the truth is unwelcome. And may thou be that person for someone else. 9May thy children, if thou hast them, know thy name not merely as an authority but as a presence. May they know the sound of thy laugh before they know the weight of thy expectations. 10May the ruins in thy life prove fertile. May what was taken from thee make room for what is coming. May the faith thou hast lost, if thou hast lost it, be findable again in the quiet, when thou art ready. 11Stephen asketh nothing of God on his own behalf that he would not ask on behalf of the reader. He hath learned that prayer openeth wider when it forgets itself. 12The Book of Stephen is finished. It was written honestly, which is the only standard its author knew how to hold himself to. 13It was written in hope that one day it may sit among the great books of the faith, not because its author was great, but because the need it answereth is real — the need of the ordinary person to find themselves somewhere in the scripture, not only among kings and prophets and apostles, but among the doubters and the grieving and the returning and the trying. 14If it findeth even one such person, it hath done enough. 15Go well. Be honest. Come back when thou wanderest. The door is open.
