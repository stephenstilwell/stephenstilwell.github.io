Chapter XII

The Benediction of Stephen

1Go now. The book is ended, but you are not. What you carry from here is yours to use or to set aside, and no word on any page can compel you. 2May you find the work that is worthy of your hands. May you love someone well and be loved in return, which is not guaranteed but is possible, and the possibility is enough to justify the attempt. 3May your doubts keep you honest and your faith keep you whole. May you grieve without being destroyed by grief, and may your joy be the kind that does not require an audience. 4Be patient with the world. It is very old and has seen much, and it changes slowly, as large things do. Be patient with yourself for the same reason. 5And if you remember nothing else from what is written here, remember this: you are not alone in any of it. Not in the dark, and not in the light, and not in the long ordinary hours between them. 6This has been the Book of Stephen. Peace be upon you who have read it.
