A Recipe by Stephen Stilwell

No-Knead Overnight Focaccia

✦ ✦ ✦

With Slow-Simmered Garlic Oil

Serves 8 ✦ 30×40 cm  or  23×33 cm ✦ Overnight + 3–4 hrs day of

A slow-fermented, hands-off focaccia built on time, not technique.
No stand mixer needed — just a bowl, a fold, and patience.

A Note on Measurement

Weight in grams is always preferred. Volumetric is provided as a backup only — baking by weight is more accurate and more consistent.

Ingredients

Dough

Bread flour, high-protein (12–14%) 500g ~4 cups (spooned & levelled)
Warm water (~35°C / 95°F) 425g ~1¾ cups
Fine sea salt 10g ~1¾ tsp
Instant or active dry yeast 4g ~1¼ tsp
Extra-virgin olive oil 30g ~2 tbsp

Pan

Extra-virgin olive oil 60g ~¼ cup

Slow-Simmered Garlic Oil

Extra-virgin olive oil 80g ~⅓ cup
Garlic cloves, thinly sliced 6 cloves
Fresh rosemary sprigs 3 sprigs
Chilli flakes (optional) 1–2g ~½ tsp

Topping

Flaky sea salt ~4g ~1 tsp

The Science

What’s happening and why.

Flour & Gluten

High-protein bread flour (12–14% protein) is essential. Gluten forms when two proteins in flour — glutenin and gliadin — bond in the presence of water. More protein means a stronger network to trap CO&sub2; from fermentation, giving you structure, chew, and an open crumb. All-purpose flour works in a pinch but produces a softer, less chewy result.

You do not need to knead this dough. Gluten develops through two mechanisms here: autolyse (simply by hydrating, flour proteins begin bonding on their own) and stretch & fold (gentle folding aligns gluten strands progressively without friction). Each set builds the network further.

Hydration

This dough is 85% hydration (425g water ÷ 500g flour). High hydration means more steam in the oven and a more open, airy, custardy crumb. The dough will feel uncomfortably sticky. That is correct. Do not add more flour — oil your hands, not the dough.

Yeast & Fermentation

4g of yeast is intentionally low for a long cold proof. Less yeast over a longer time allows enzymes to break down starches into more complex sugars, and organic acids (lactic, acetic) to build flavour depth — a slow, even rise without overproofing. Cold fermentation slows yeast activity but keeps enzymatic activity ticking along. This is where real flavour is built. 18 hours is the sweet spot.

Salt

Salt tightens the gluten network, controls fermentation speed, and is critical for flavour. Do not reduce it. Add it after the yeast is dissolved — direct contact between undiluted salt and yeast can inhibit or kill it.

Oil in the Pan

The generous pool of olive oil on the pan base is not just anti-stick. It essentially fries the bottom of the focaccia in the oven, creating the deeply golden, crispy underside that defines good focaccia. Do not reduce it.

Method

Day One — Evening

About 2–2½ hours, mostly waiting.

1 Mix

In a large bowl, whisk together 425g warm water and 4g yeast. If using active dry yeast: wait 5–10 minutes until frothy. If using instant yeast: proceed immediately.

Add 10g salt and 30g olive oil. Stir briefly. Add 500g flour all at once. Mix with a stiff spatula or your hand until no dry flour remains — about 2 minutes. The dough will look rough, shaggy, and sticky. That is exactly right. Cover tightly.

2 Bulk Fermentation & Stretch & Fold

Leave covered at room temperature for 30 minutes. Then perform your first set of stretch & folds.

How to Stretch & Fold

Wet your hand. Reach under one side of the dough, stretch it upward as far as it will go without tearing, fold it over the top. Rotate the bowl 90°. Repeat. Four folds equals one set.

Perform 3–4 sets, one every 30 minutes. You will feel the dough tighten and smooth with each round. After the final fold it should be noticeably smoother, slightly puffed, and more elastic.

3 Cold Proof Overnight

Cover the bowl tightly and refrigerate for 12–24 hours. 18 hours is ideal. The dough will look less dramatically puffy than a warm proof — that is fine. It will come alive in the pan.

Day Two — Baking Day

4 Make the Garlic Oil

Pour 80g olive oil into a small saucepan. Add 6 thinly sliced garlic cloves and 3 rosemary sprigs. Place over the lowest possible heat — the oil should barely shimmer, never sizzle or bubble aggressively.

Cook for 20–25 minutes until garlic is pale golden, completely tender, and sweet-smelling. This is confit, not frying. Add chilli flakes in the final 5 minutes if using. Remove from heat, discard rosemary stems, and let cool completely. The garlic slices are a topping.

5 Pan & Second Proof

Pour 60g olive oil into your pan, coating the entire base. Scrape the cold dough from the fridge into the pan. Flip it once gently so both sides are coated in oil. Do not press it out yet — let it rest, loosely covered, at room temperature for 2–4 hours until relaxed, puffy, and filling most of the pan. If it springs back when you try to stretch it, it is not ready.

6 Dimple, Top & Final Rest

Preheat oven to 230°C / 450°F (fan/convection preferred). Drizzle half the cooled garlic oil over the surface. With well-oiled fingers, press firmly all the way to the pan base in a rhythmic motion across the whole surface. These are your dimples — they hold oil, anchor toppings, and prevent large bubbles from ballooning.

Scatter confit garlic slices across the top and press them lightly in. Sprinkle with ~4g flaky sea salt. Let rest uncovered for 20 minutes while the oven finishes heating.

7 Bake

Bake on the lower-middle rack for 20–25 minutes. The top should be deep golden. The underside is your real indicator — lift a corner with a spatula: you want bronze, not blond. Pale focaccia is underbaked focaccia. Rotate the pan halfway through if your oven has hot spots.

8 Rest & Finish

Immediately drizzle the remaining garlic oil over the hot surface. It will sizzle and soak in. This is the final layer of flavour. After 5 minutes, slide the focaccia onto a wire rack. Rest for at least 15 minutes before cutting — this sets the crumb and keeps the crust crisp rather than letting it steam itself soft.

Notes & Variations

Can I Speed This Up?

Yes. Skip the cold proof. After your stretch & folds, let the dough bulk ferment at room temperature for 4 hours total, then pan and proof for 1–2 hours before baking. Use 6g yeast instead of 4g. Flavour will be milder but still very good.

Toppings Beyond Garlic
  • Cherry tomatoes, halved, pressed cut-side-up into the dimples
  • Black or Castelvetrano olives
  • Caramelised onion with thyme
  • Thinly sliced potato, par-blanched, with rosemary
  • Just excellent olive oil and flaky salt (the purist’s choice)
Storage

Best eaten same day. To revive leftovers: reheat slices in a dry skillet over medium heat — this restores the crispy underside. Freezes excellently: wrap slices tightly, reheat from frozen in a 180°C / 350°F oven for 10 minutes.
